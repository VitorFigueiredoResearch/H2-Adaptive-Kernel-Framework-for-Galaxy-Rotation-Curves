3. RAR PHYSICS CONVENTION
--------------------------
Model form: McGaugh, Lelli & Schombert (2016) RAR interpolation function

  g_obs = g_bar / (1 - exp(-sqrt(g_bar / g_dag)))

where:
  g_bar  = baryonic centripetal acceleration = V_bar^2(r) / r
  g_dag  = fundamental MOND acceleration scale (FIXED at 1.2e-10 m/s^2)
  g_obs  = predicted total centripetal acceleration
  V_tot  = sqrt(g_obs * r)  [in km/s if r in kpc and g in km^2/s^2/kpc]

Unit conversion:
  g in m/s^2 to km^2/s^2/kpc: multiply by 3.086e16 (1 kpc in m)
  g_dag in working units: 1.2e-10 m/s^2 * 3.086e16 m/kpc = 3703.2 km^2/s^2/kpc

No free DM parameters. Only free parameters: Ydisk, Ybul, D, inc.
g_dag is NOT a free parameter in the Li et al. RAR fits — it is fixed.

4. PERTURBATION SCHEME
-----------------------
Since g_dag is the fundamental MOND scale (not fitted), bounded perturbations
of g_dag test the sensitivity of inner-region scatter to the MOND acceleration scale.
This is the direct MOND analog of bounded NFW parameter perturbations.

Primary perturbation: g_dag variation
  Magnitudes: +/-10%, +/-20%
  Motivation: g_dag ~ 1.2e-10 m/s^2 has a ~10% uncertainty in the literature
              (Lelli+2017: 1.20 +/- 0.02 (stat) +/- 0.24 (syst))

Secondary perturbation: Ydisk variation
  Magnitudes: +/-10%, +/-20%
  Motivation: directly comparable to NFW M/L perturbations; constrains
              how much the inner-region scatter responds to stellar mass changes

8. AMBIGUITIES AND NOTES
-------------------------
- The RAR as fitted in Li et al. treats g_dag as FIXED (not a free parameter).
  Therefore, perturbations of g_dag are "out-of-fit" variations rather than
  parameter error propagation — this is intentional and matches the NFW scheme
  (which also applies perturbations rather than propagating fit errors).
- The RAR model velocity at each radius is derived from baryonic velocity
  components scaled by the best-fit Ydisk and Ybul from the RAR fit.
- No ambiguity in interpolation function: the McGaugh+2016 RAR formula is
  the only function used in Li et al. 2020 (no "simple" vs "standard" variants).
