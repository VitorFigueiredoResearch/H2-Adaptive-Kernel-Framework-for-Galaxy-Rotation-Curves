2. MAIN REVIEWER OBJECTIONS (from a MOND-informed reader)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OBJECTION 1 (most important):
  "g_dag is a universal physical constant in MOND theory, not an adjustable
   model parameter. Varying g_dag is not a valid MOND perturbation — it is
   equivalent to varying G or c. Calling this 'bounded MOND adaptation' is
   a category error."

  ASSESSMENT: This is a VALID and serious objection. Li et al. (2020) fix
  g_dag at 1.2e-10 m/s² and do not treat it as a fit parameter. The
  Lelli+2017 systematic uncertainty (~20%) reflects measurement uncertainty
  in g_dag, not freedom within MOND theory.

  REQUIRED MANUSCRIPT WORDING:
    Do NOT say: "MOND is bounded/adaptive."
    Do NOT say: "We perturb MOND parameters."
    USE INSTEAD: "We test the sensitivity of the inner-region scatter metric
    to bounded variations in the RAR acceleration scale g_dag, treating these
    variations as a sensitivity analysis around the adopted fiducial value
    rather than as parameter freedom within MOND theory."

OBJECTION 2:
  "Ydisk is already a free parameter in the RAR fit. Perturbing it by ±10%
   around the best-fit value is just testing how much re-fitting Ydisk would
   matter — it is not an 'out-of-fit' test."

  ASSESSMENT: PARTIALLY VALID. Ydisk perturbations are not refits (Ydisk
  is displaced from its best-fit value without adjusting other parameters).
  This is the same non-refit scheme used for NFW. However, the reviewer's
  point that Ydisk is already a fitted parameter is correct and distinguishes
  these perturbations from the g_dag ones.

  REQUIRED MANUSCRIPT WORDING:
    "Ydisk perturbations represent controlled displacements from the Li et al.
    best-fit stellar mass ratio, testing scatter sensitivity without refitting.
    These are directly analogous to the NFW mass-to-light perturbations."

OBJECTION 3:
  "Only one MOND interpolation function is tested (McGaugh+2016). The result
   could change with the 'simple' or 'standard' interpolation functions."

  ASSESSMENT: VALID limitation but clearly bounded. The Li et al. data only
  contain fits for the McGaugh+2016 form. The manuscript should state this
  explicitly and leave interpolation-function comparison to future work.

OBJECTION 4:
  "The comparison conflates MOND sensitivity with RAR sensitivity. MOND
   and the RAR are related but distinct frameworks."

  ASSESSMENT: VALID distinction. The paper should refer consistently to
  "the RAR/MOND framework as implemented in Li et al. (2020)" and not
  claim to test "MOND" as a full gravitational theory.

3. IS g_dag VARIATION A VALID BOUNDED SENSITIVITY TEST?
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

VERDICT: Yes, as a sensitivity analysis. No, as a MOND parameter test.

The g_dag variation IS valid as:
  - A test of how sensitive inner-region scatter is to the adopted
    acceleration scale, within the range allowed by measurement uncertainty
  - Directly analogous in STRUCTURE to the NFW mass/concentration variation:
    both displace a key model parameter from its reference value
  - Scientifically honest: the ±20% range is grounded in the Lelli+2017
    systematic uncertainty

The g_dag variation is NOT valid if framed as:
  - "MOND has parameter freedom here"
  - "We are testing bounded MOND adaptation"
  - "This is how MOND responds to perturbation"

CORRECT FRAMING: "g_dag sensitivity analysis within the RAR implementation"

4. YDISK PERTURBATION DEFENSIBILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Ydisk perturbations are the MOST DEFENSIBLE part of the MOND analysis:
  - Ydisk represents genuine parameter uncertainty (stellar population models
    have ~30% systematic M/L uncertainty)
  - The ±10%/±20% perturbation is conservative relative to real uncertainty
  - Directly comparable to NFW M/L perturbations (same parameter, same role)
  - The scatter sensitivity to Ydisk can legitimately be called a
    "bounded sensitivity test" without MOND-theory framing

MEDIAN MOND max|Δσ| from Ydisk-only perturbations should be reported
separately in the manuscript as the most reviewer-robust result.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

YES, with mandatory wording restrictions:

  The MOND/RAR analysis is technically sound and defensible as a
  "bounded sensitivity analysis of the RAR implementation" from
