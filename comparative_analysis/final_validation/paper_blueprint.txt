6. CENTRAL CLAIM (one paragraph, cautious journal language)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

"We present a comparative diagnostic test of inner-region rotation curve
scatter sensitivity across three rotation curve model frameworks — an
adaptive nonlocal kernel (H2), standard NFW dark matter halo fits, and
the Radial Acceleration Relation (RAR/MOND) — applied uniformly to a
74-galaxy subset of the SPARC survey. Using a harmonized inner-region
scatter metric (log10 velocity residual RMS at R < 0.5 R_max), and
applying identical bounded perturbations (±10%, ±20%) to the principal
model parameters in each framework, we find that the three frameworks
exhibit distinct scatter sensitivity patterns: the H2 adaptive kernel
produces near-neutral inner-region scatter (median |Δσ| ≈ 0.0005 dex);
the RAR/MOND implementation produces intermediate sensitivity (median
max|Δσ| ≈ 0.032 dex); and NFW halo parameter perturbations produce the
largest sensitivity (median max|Δσ| ≈ 0.055 dex), with a strong
anti-correlation between scatter sensitivity and baryonic dominance
(Spearman ρ = −0.90, p < 10^−25). We interpret this regime dependence
as consistent with a structural effect in which bounded perturbations
of a subdominant quadrature-added velocity term have limited leverage
in baryon-dominated inner regions. These results characterize structural
differences in inner-region scatter response across the tested
implementations and provide a diagnostic baseline for future model comparison."
