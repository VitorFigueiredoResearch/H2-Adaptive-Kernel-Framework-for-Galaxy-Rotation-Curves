Q2. What is still risky?
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RISK 1 — H2 coverage gap (MODERATE risk):
  The log10 dex harmonic reconstruction covers only 9 of 80 H2 fleet
  galaxies. The comparison table uses inferred ~0 values for the other 71.
  A referee may demand the H2 result be reproduced in the harmonized metric
  for the full fleet. This is a legitimate and foreseeable request.
  MITIGATION: State clearly in the manuscript. The 9-galaxy exact result
  (all < 0.003 dex) strongly supports the broader claim, and the H2 paper
  documents the full fleet result separately.

RISK 2 — g_dag framing (MODERATE risk):
  Any MOND-knowledgeable referee will object to treating g_dag as a
  variable parameter. This is not a fatal objection — the analysis is
  framed as a sensitivity test — but an imprecise manuscript could
  provoke a rejection on framing grounds alone.
  MITIGATION: Use only the exact wording in mond_defensibility_check.txt.
  Never say "MOND perturbation" or "MOND adaptation."

RISK 3 — H2 is not a published/peer-reviewed model yet (LOW-MODERATE risk):
  The comparative paper references H2 results from a manuscript that has
  not been accepted. MNRAS may require H2 to be peer-reviewed or
  cite the preprint explicitly.
  MITIGATION: Frame H2 as "an adaptive nonlocal kernel model (Figueiredo 2026,
  in preparation)" and make the comparison self-contained. The paper's
  contribution stands independently of H2's publication status.

RISK 4 — MOND regime direction (LOW risk):
  MOND shows smaller sensitivity for DM-dominated galaxies than baryon-dominated
  ones — opposite direction from NFW. This is physically explainable but may
  confuse readers expecting a uniform picture.
  MITIGATION: Explain the deep-MOND vs Newtonian regime physics (2 sentences).

   perturbation magnitudes, and sample.

6. "Varying g_dag is a valid test of bounded MOND adaptation" — g_dag is a
   universal constant in MOND theory, not an adjustable parameter.
